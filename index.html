Research Experience

The imaginary phase problem in the gravitational path integral

completing
Supervisor: Prof. Amin Faraji Astaneh

In the gravitational path integral, the saddle point approximation introduces an imaginary contribution—Polchinski's phase—which complicates state counting. Interestingly, this aligns with the structure of von Neumann algebras in gravitational regions. In de Sitter gravity, adopting an observer’s perspective transforms the region’s algebra into a type II von Neumann algebra, suggesting a potential link between Polchinski's phase and state counting.

Selberg Zeta in Flat Space Cosmologies

Uncomplete
Supervisor: Prof. Reza Fareghbal

A generalization of the Selberg zeta function to flat space cosmologies, exploring connections between number theory and quantum gravity in non-AdS spacetimes. In a series of papers, using certain conjectures about the Selberg zeta function, researchers were able to derive the gravitational partition function for flat space and BTZ black holes. Our approach, however, is based on a more rigorous definition grounded in number theory, allowing us to obtain the gravitational partition function for these geometries without relying on conjectures.

Topological Aspects of Berry Phase

Uncomplete
Supervisor: Prof. Siamak Sadat Gousheh

The Berry phase captures the geometric and topological properties of a quantum system’s wavefunction under adiabatic evolution. Beyond a simple phase factor, it encodes topological information that underlies phenomena like the quantum Hall effect, topological insulators, and geometric magnetism, revealing how global system properties shape observable quantum behavior.

Research Interests

As a Master’s student, I have developed a strong interest in topics such as the role of the observer in de Sitter space and its influence on Polchinski’s phase, the dynamics of JT gravity, and aspects of Topological Field Theory.

JT Gravity

Jackiw–Teitelboim (JT) Gravity is a simple yet powerful model of two-dimensional quantum gravity. Despite its minimal setup, it captures deep features of black hole physics, spacetime dynamics, and holography. JT gravity has become a central playground for exploring ideas like quantum chaos, information loss, and the AdS/CFT correspondence, offering insights that extend far beyond its deceptively small world.

Observers in Quantum Gravity

In de Sitter spacetime, the presence of a cosmological horizon means that different observers generally have access to different regions of the universe. This makes “reality” observer-dependent: what one observer can measure or even define as a quantum state may be inaccessible to another. Such observer effects are central to understanding entropy, information, and quantum fields in dS space — and they highlight the deep puzzle of how to formulate a consistent theory of quantum gravity in an expanding universe.

Algebraic Quantum Field Theory

Algebraic Quantum Field Theory (AQFT) is a rigorous framework for understanding quantum fields, emphasizing the algebraic structures underlying observables and their relations rather than relying solely on field operators or Lagrangians. In AQFT, physical quantities in a given region of spacetime are organized into von Neumann algebras, allowing for precise treatment of locality, causality, and symmetries. This approach provides deep insights into phenomena such as entanglement in quantum fields, thermal properties of horizons, and the mathematical foundations of quantum statistics, making it a powerful tool for both high-energy physics and quantum information theory.
