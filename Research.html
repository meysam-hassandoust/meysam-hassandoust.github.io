My Research Interests

JT Gravity

Jackiw–Teitelboim (JT) Gravity is a simple yet powerful model of two-dimensional quantum gravity. Despite its minimal setup, it captures deep features of black hole physics, spacetime dynamics, and holography. JT gravity has become a central playground for exploring ideas like quantum chaos, information loss, and the AdS/CFT correspondence, offering insights that extend far beyond its deceptively small world.

Observers in Quantum Gravity

In de Sitter spacetime, the presence of a cosmological horizon means that different observers generally have access to different regions of the universe. This makes “reality” observer-dependent: what one observer can measure or even define as a quantum state may be inaccessible to another. Such observer effects are central to understanding entropy, information, and quantum fields in dS space — and they highlight the deep puzzle of how to formulate a consistent theory of quantum gravity in an expanding universe.

Topological Field Theory

A Topological Field Theory is a special type of quantum field theory where physical quantities do not depend on distances or shapes, but only on the topology of the space. This means the theory is insensitive to local geometric details and instead captures global, topological features — like how many holes a surface has. TFTs provide a deep link between physics and pure mathematics, with applications ranging from knot invariants to quantum computing and the study of quantum gravity.
