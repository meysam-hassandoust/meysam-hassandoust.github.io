Real Observer and Polchinski's phase

One interesting problem I’ve been exploring is how an observer affects the Polchinski phase. This phase appears when we consider the saddle point approximation up to second order, and it introduces an imaginary factor in calculating the partition function of a sphere. At first, this seems to break unitarity, which might remind you of swampland-type issues. However, Prof. Maldacena showed that by carefully defining what we mean by an observer in de Sitter space as Chandrasekaran, Longo, Penington and Witten suggested this imaginary phase can actually be removed, though it introduces a negative sign in the density of states.

What makes this even more fascinating is that considering an observer is like adding a microcanonical constraint to the system. From the perspective of topological field theory (TFT), the observer, using their own proper time, acts like an operator connecting their two horizons. From this point of view, it seems that the horizon state as seen by the observer naturally corresponds to the Hartle–Hawking state.
